How to get to Mérida

Mérida, the capital of Yucatán, is easily accessible by plane, bus, or car. The main airport is Manuel Crescencio Rejón International Airport (MID), located about 15–25 minutes from downtown.

By plane

  • Direct flights to Mérida (MID) are available from several hubs (especially from the U.S.).
  • From Europe / international travelers: the most common connections are via Mexico City (MEX) or Cancún (CUN).
Tip: If you arrive in Cancún, you can reach Mérida comfortably by ADO bus in ~4 hours.

From Cancún by bus

  • Company: ADO (very frequent departures)
  • Typical duration: ~4h (depending on the service)
  • Arrival: ADO terminal in Mérida (city center / close to downtown)

From Cancún by car

  • Travel time: ~3.5–4h
  • Toll highway available (recommended for comfort)

Local transportation in Mérida

The historic center is very walkable, and ride options are widely available.

  • UBER or DiDi service (easy and reliable)
  • Local taxis (taxímetro)
  • Local buses “Va y Ven” (requires a bus card) — cost approx.: 0.70 USD
Note: For “Va y Ven”, you usually need a rechargeable card. If you plan to use buses, buy the card early and keep small cash for convenience.

What to do in Mérida during evenings

Mérida offers free cultural events almost every night, mainly in the historic center. Times and locations can occasionally change, so feel free to double-check locally.

Monday

Vaquería Yucateca (Traditional regional dance of Yucatán)
Location: City House (Palacio Municipal)
Time: 21:00 hrs

Tuesday

“Remembranzas Musicales” (Popular music from the 1940s, mostly Danzón)
Location: Santiago’s Park, Downtown
Time: 20:30 hrs

Wednesday

Recorrido por el Cementerio de Mérida (Guided tour to the cemetery of Mérida)
Time: 20:00 hrs

“Noche de las Culturas” (Cultural Nights)
Meeting point: Francisco de Montejo / Montejo’s House (Downtown)
Time: 20:30 hrs

Thursday

Serenata (Serenade)
Location: Santa Lucía Park
Time: 21:00 hrs

“En el Corazón de Mérida”
When: Thursday to Saturday — 20:00 to 02:00 hrs
A full street in the city center is closed so that restaurants, musical groups, local artists and artisans can exhibit in a festive open-air atmosphere.

Friday

Video mapping “Piedras Sagradas”
Location: Cathedral of Mérida
Time: 20:30 hrs

Saturday

Mexican Night
Location: End of Paseo Montejo
Time: Starting at 20:00 hrs

Mayan ball game “Pok Ta Pok”
Location: Beside the Cathedral of Mérida
Time: 20:30 hrs
